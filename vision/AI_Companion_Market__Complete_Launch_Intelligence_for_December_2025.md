**Critical November 2025 change**: Character.AI **banned all minor users** following multiple wrongful death lawsuits, eliminating its dominant Gen Z demographic (51% of users were 18-24). This creates immediate market share opportunity.

## Acquisition strategies require platform-specific playbooks

### TikTok delivers the best CAC in 2025

Replika's internal data shows TikTok delivers CPIs **70-80% cheaper than Facebook** and **40% cheaper than Snapchat**. More importantly, TikTok campaigns generate a **30% organic download halo effect** from content going semi-viral.

**Loora AI case study** (AI English tutor) achieved 25,000 installs in under 2 months with **67% lower CPI** using Spark Ads amplifying niche creator content. The formula: partner with micro-influencers through TikTok Creator Marketplace, provide guidelines but creative freedom, then boost authentic-feeling content.

**Viral content formats that perform**:
- Demo conversations showing impressive AI responses
- Emotional moments (AI providing support)
- "Wait, this AI can do THAT?" curiosity hooks
- 15-30 second videos with hooks in first 3 seconds

### Reddit drives high-intent organic traffic

Character.AI's **1.6M-member subreddit** generates substantial organic discovery without paid advertising. Reddit presence in Google search results increased **191% in 2024**, making community-building a long-term SEO play.

**Implementation**: Create branded subreddit early, build karma through genuine engagement before promotion, provide value-first content, follow community rules strictly (self-promotion gets banned).

### Product Hunt is credibility, not scale

The January 2024 algorithm change means only **10% of launches now get featured**. If featured: expect 1,000-5,000 visitors and 10-150 signups. One case study showed 612 upvotes and #1 position resulting in just **1 paying customer**. Use Product Hunt for the DR 91 backlink and social proof, not primary acquisition.

## Regulatory environment demands immediate compliance action

### California SB 243 creates urgent deadline

**Effective January 1, 2026**—less than one month away. This first-in-nation companion chatbot law requires:

- Clear disclosure that chatbot is AI when users could be misled
- Suicide/self-harm detection with crisis service referrals (988 hotline integration)
- For minors: 3-hour break reminders, explicit AI disclosure, NSFW content prevention
- **Private right of action** for anyone suffering "injury in fact"

The private right of action is the critical risk—it enables lawsuits from any harmed user without waiting for regulatory enforcement.

### Active lawsuits establish dangerous precedents

**Garcia v. Character Technologies** (October 2024): Federal judge **denied motion to dismiss**, allowing product liability claims against AI chatbots to proceed. The case—involving a 14-year-old's suicide after forming a romantic relationship with a chatbot—establishes that AI companions may be treated as "products" subject to strict liability. First Amendment defenses failed.

Additional wrongful death lawsuits against Character.AI (Peralta family, Texas teen) and OpenAI (Raine, Shamblin) are proceeding. Discovery will expose internal safety decisions—documenting safety-by-design is now essential.

### EU AI Act creates compliance timeline through 2027

Text-based emotional AI companions likely fall outside "emotion recognition" definitions (which require biometric data), but transparency obligations under **Article 50** apply: users must be informed when interacting with AI. Full high-risk rules take effect August 2026.

**GDPR precedent**: Italy fined Replika **€5 million** in May 2025 for inadequate transparency, no legal basis for processing, and no age verification.

### State-level patchwork is expanding
